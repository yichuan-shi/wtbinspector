Abstract

The rise of the renewable energy industry has propelled the growth of the wind power sector, escalating demands on the operation and maintenance of wind turbines. Recent advancements have integrated UAVs and automated algorithms, enhancing the efficiency of automated wind turbine blade inspection. Nonetheless, challenges persist in this field. Primarily, critical on-site parameters of stationary turbines such as turbine yaw, blade rest angle and tip position cannot be pre-obtained from the wind farm operator. Existing overviews however struggle to accurately estimate turbine tilt angle and blade tip position and have limited applicability for blade rest angle estimation. Additionally, neglect of camera orientation during blade surface tracking degrades imaging effect. Moreover, existing pipelines focus on optimizing image quality after inspection rather than in real time during acquisition, an approach that struggles to recover details lost during the imaging process. To tackle these challenges, we introduce WTBInspector: a fully automated UAV-based wind turbine blade inspection system. This system fulfills three key functions: extracting the turbine structure and estimating complete pose parameters, maintaining camera alignment with the blade surface to ensure orthogonal imaging during blade tracking, and dynamically enhancing image quality in real time during image acquisition. Integrating hardware and software and a ground monitoring application, the system enables efficient and high-quality inspection. Over 600 field inspections have been conducted on 10 distinct wind turbine models across 5 operational wind farms. These inspections covered complex hilly terrain and up to 10 m/s winds, achieving a 99.98% blade surface coverage rate. Except for unsuitability in extreme weather such as heavy rain or dense fog, the field tests confirm that WTBInspector exhibits robustness, applicability and effectiveness.

Overview

Framework

System framework. Utilizing the onboard sensors, the point cloud and image, along with the drone position and attitude, are acquired. The Automated Wind Turbine Parameter Estimation (AWPE) module estimates the wind turbine parameters, followed by the Gimbal-Oriented Blade Surface Tracking (GOST) module for blade surface tracking, and the Real-Time Image Quality Optimization (RIQO) module for real-time image quality optimization. The resulting drone velocity command, gimbal attitude command, and camera parameters combine to enable blade surface inspection and image acquisition.

Stage 1

PAPT: Pose-Aware Projectile Trajectory Prediction

Synchronized RGB-D streams are first used to estimate 3D human keypoints in real time. The recent motion of the throwing joint is smoothed with a pre-release trajectory model, and multiple plausible release instants are detected from the joint’s velocity and acceleration dynamics. For each candidate release moment, the system predicts a post-release projectile trajectory using a physics-based motion model.

Stage 2

UAD: Uncertainty-Aware Dodging

Because both the release timing and the predicted trajectory are uncertain, each candidate projectile path is surrounded by an expanding ivory-shaped uncertainty region. A collision threat is identified when the UAV’s current position or planned trajectory intersects this region, enabling conservative but timely risk assessment.

Optimization

Threat-Aware Motion Planning

The UAV trajectory is parameterized by MINCO and optimized in a GCOPTER-style framework with smoothness, feasibility, obstacle, and time objectives. On top of these standard terms, we introduce two dodging-oriented penalties: a surviving-trajectory penalty that pushes the UAV away from all still-threatening candidate projectile paths, and a relative-velocity penalty that encourages safer avoidance directions.

Key Advantage

Long-Range, Low-Latency Onboard Response

By leveraging human pose instead of relying only on direct projectile appearance, the framework enables earlier threat anticipation, longer effective detection range, and real-time onboard dodging using only an RGB-D camera, without requiring expensive event cameras or heavy perception hardware.

In our experiments, the perception module achieves an effective detection distance of 6.0 m with an average latency of 26.4 ms on CPU-only hardware. For 3D keypoint tracking, the position RMSE is 0.037 m and the velocity RMSE is 0.154 m/s, supporting reliable early warning and real-time evasive motion in diverse real-world attack scenarios.
